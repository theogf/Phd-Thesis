The natural question following the work on P\'olya-Gamma variables and other augmentation works is what kind of likelihoods can be augmented, what are the properties needed?
This article partially answers this question by giving a set of constraints where the likelihood will be guaranteed to be augmented.
It also goes further by providing an automatic algorithm for the \ac{CAVI} and Gibbs sampling algorithm
